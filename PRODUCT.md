# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Rootine OS is built primarily for the owner and a small circle of roughly 5-10 similar power users: people who want one private, high-density operating system for everyday life, work, training, money, planning, and personal admin. They use it repeatedly through the day, often to scan state quickly, decide what matters next, and keep separate life domains from becoming scattered across many tools.

## Product Purpose

Rootine OS exists to be a personal command center: Planner, Goals, Sport, Diet, Finance, Travel, Office, Work, and Notes in one coherent shell. Success means the user can open the app and immediately understand the day, the active obligations, the next actions, and the state of each life module without feeling like they are inside a generic corporate dashboard or a blank modular workspace.

## Brand Personality

Premium, personal, focused.

The interface should feel like a polished private operating room for life operations: calm, exact, dense where density helps, and visually refined enough to make repeated use feel satisfying rather than administrative.

## Anti-references

Rootine OS should not resemble a corporate dashboard: no generic SaaS analytics layout, no boardroom KPI wall, no sterile enterprise admin tone.

Rootine OS should not resemble Notion: no blank-document workspace feeling, no loose block-based canvas, no generic productivity-template aesthetic, and no sense that the app is a collection of undifferentiated pages.

## Design Principles

1. Private operating system, not generic workspace.
2. Premium density: show enough information to be useful without turning the surface into noise.
3. One raster across modules: every tab should feel like part of the same machine.
4. State before decoration: visual treatment must help the user understand status, urgency, progress, and next action.
5. Personal craft over enterprise neutrality.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG AA target is required for this product. The priority is a beautiful, premium interface for a narrow group of users without known accessibility constraints.

The app should still preserve basic product quality: readable text, sensible hit targets, keyboard-friendly controls where natural, and motion that feels controlled rather than distracting.
